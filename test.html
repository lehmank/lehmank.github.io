Research

Cosmic Voids

Cosmic voids open up a new window to constrain cosmology and test General Relativity (GR). As voids generally represent regions of the Universe of low density, they are particularly sensitive to effects of neutrinos, possible devia- tions from GR and become dark energy dominated at earlier times. Further cosmological tests are possible via redshift-space distortions, the Alcock- Paczy ́nski test or void lensing. Not only do voids as cosmological probes show great potential, but with upcoming surveys such as LSST and EUCLID it will be of utter importance to maximize the information extracted from these missions. Cosmic voids are already “freely” available within this data without the need for any additional observations. This makes their analysis an invaluable cosmological tool, complementary to standard probes like clusters and galaxies.
